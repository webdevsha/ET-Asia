1. Goals and Objectives

The Effective Thesis Accelerator is an 8-week program designed to guide students from early thesis and career ideas to leaving with:

  1. Thesis: A well-developed, high-impact thesis proposal with a clear Theory of Change (ToC) ideally with expert collaboration, connection or interviews.
  2. Career: A clear high-impact career plan which aligns with your thesis.
  3. Self: Greater motivation, agency, and self-awareness towards defining your vision for impact, and building skills towards executing on it.

2. Program Benefits

  • Fast track your high-impact thesis. Develop a thesis that aligns with your vision for impact on the world, your career path, and your community.
  • Produce public outputs. Produce public outputs, i.e. articles, blogposts, presentations, that contribute to your journey towards impact. Top-performing applicants may be invited to share their work on ET's public platform.
  • Pathway to Effective Thesis Fellowship. High-performing applicants may be invited to become Effective Thesis Fellows for deeper mentorship and hands-on support in executing a high-impact thesis after the accelerator ends.

3. Weekly Overview

The program is structured over eight weeks with four weeks of structured guidance, and four weeks of focused thesis development.

  • Weeks 1-4 Modules and Guidance: The first four weeks focus on learning through modules and worksheets - helping you clarify your values, interests and high-impact problem areas.
  • Weeks 5-8 Thesis Development: The last four weeks are dedicated to active thesis development, collaboration, and applying your learning to produce a concrete, high-impact research proposal.
Week Description
Week 1: Foundations to Impact In Week 1, you will explore what a high-impact thesis means and engage with frameworks for thinking about impact. We will explore the Theory of Change framework and high-impact career planning to help align your thesis with your vision for impact - for the world, you, and your surrounding community.
Week 2: Problem Mapping This week focuses on understanding your chosen problem in depth. You will examine why you want to work on this problem, considering its importance, neglectedness, and tractability. You will map the system behind the problem, including root causes and potential bottlenecks.
Week 3: Pathways to Impact & Outreach Week 3 is about understanding the current landscape of potential pathways to impact for your problem area. You will perform stakeholder mapping, identify who is working on what, and begin reaching out to potential experts & collaborators.
Week 4: My Pathway to Impact In Week 4, you will refine your specific research question and Theory of Change for your chosen thesis. The goal is to transform your broader interest into a concrete, actionable research question that is feasible, high-impact, and aligned with your long-term goals.
Week 5 to 7: Thesis Development These three weeks will be dedicated to developing your thesis in depth. You will perform expert interviews, finalise your research question, define aims and methodology, and draft a literature review.
Week 8: Final Presentation In the final week, you will present your completed research proposal and Theory of Change to peers and experts, gaining live feedback and practice in communicating your work. By the end of the program, you will have a polished high-impact research proposal, a short presentation, a 6-month action plan, and ideally a secured mentor or collaborator to support your next steps.

4. Program Structure

You'll follow a weekly rhythm designed for around 3-5 hours per week. The program consists of:

📋 Self-Directed Modules and Worksheets

Work through readings, exercises, and projects each week. You'll submit your work for feedback from peers and the ET Team.

🗣️ Weekly Peer Group Meetings

Each week, you'll be assigned a peer group to meet and collaborate with on projects or for personalised feedback.

🎉 Biweekly Whole Cohort Discussions

Every other Thursday (~1.5 hours), the full cohort will meet to discuss readings and projects, reflect on progress, and share insights.

🖥️ Optional Advisor Meetings

You can register for optional advisor sessions to discuss your progress, resolve uncertainties, or receive additional guidance and support.

No! We welcome students at all levels who are passionate about making an impact. Whether you're an undergraduate just starting your thesis journey or a graduate student looking to pivot your research direction, our program is designed to meet you where you are.

The program is designed for 3-5 hours per week over 8 weeks. This includes self-directed learning, peer group meetings, and optional advisor sessions. We understand you have other commitments and have structured the program to be manageable alongside your regular coursework.
